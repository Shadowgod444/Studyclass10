Class 10 Science - Ultimate Night Mode Study Guide

Our Environment
  • Ecosystem: Interaction between biotic (living) and abiotic (non-living) components.
  • Food Chains: Energy flow from producers to consumers and decomposers.
  • Biological Magnification: Harmful substances (e.g., pesticides) increase in concentration along the food chain, affecting apex predators most severely.
  • Waste Management: Methods: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle; importance of composting and segregation.

Important Questions:

  • Explain biological magnification with an example.
  • Describe the role of decomposers in an ecosystem.
  • List three ways to manage waste effectively.
Chemical Reactions and Equations
  • Signs of Chemical Reaction: Color change, gas production, precipitate formation, temperature change.
  • Balancing Equations: Conservation of atoms on both sides.
  • Types: Combination, Decomposition, Displacement, Double Displacement, Redox.
  • Redox Reactions: Simultaneous oxidation and reduction.

Important Questions:

  • Write the balanced chemical equation for the reaction between sodium and water.
  • Differentiate between combination and displacement reactions with examples.
  • What is a redox reaction? Give an example.
Acids, Bases, and Salts
  • Properties of Acids: Sour taste, pH < 7, turn blue litmus red.
  • Properties of Bases: Bitter taste, slippery feel, pH > 7, turn red litmus blue.
  • Neutralization Reaction: Acid + Base → Salt + Water.
  • Common Salts: Sodium chloride, baking soda, washing soda.
  • pH Scale: Measuring acidity/alkalinity (0-14).

Important Questions:

  • What is neutralization? Explain with chemical equation.
  • How is the pH of a solution related to the concentration of H⁺ ions?
  • Name important uses of baking soda and washing soda.
Life Processes
  • Nutrition: Autotrophic (photosynthesis) and heterotrophic modes.
  • Respiration: Process releasing energy by oxidation of food. Aerobic and anaerobic types.
  • Circulation: Heart pumps blood through arteries and veins, delivering oxygen & nutrients.
  • Excretion: Removal of metabolic wastes by kidneys, lungs, skin.

Important Questions:

  • Describe the process of photosynthesis with chemical equation.
  • Explain the difference between aerobic and anaerobic respiration.
  • What is the role of kidneys in excretion?
Light – Reflection and Refraction
  • Reflection: Light bouncing back; laws of reflection (angle of incidence = angle of reflection).
  • Mirrors: Plane (virtual, same size), concave (real/virtual, magnified/reduced), convex (virtual, diminished).
  • Refraction: Bending of light passing through different mediums.
  • Lenses: Convex (converges light), concave (diverges light).

Important Questions:

  • State and explain the laws of reflection.
  • What types of images are formed by concave and convex mirrors?
  • Define refraction and give one everyday example.
The Human Eye and the Colourful World
  • Structure of Eye: Cornea, iris, pupil, lens, retina, optic nerve.
  • Defects: Myopia, Hypermetropia, Presbyopia, with corrections using lenses.
  • Dispersion: Splitting of white light into 7 colors by a prism.
  • Scattering: Explains blue sky and reddish sunrise/sunset.
  • Rainbow: Formed by refraction, reflection, and dispersion in water droplets.

Important Questions:

  • Describe the structure of the human eye with functions of its parts.
  • Explain myopia and its correction.
  • How is a rainbow formed?
